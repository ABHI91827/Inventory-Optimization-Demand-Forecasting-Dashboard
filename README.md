Inventory Optimization & Demand Forecasting Dashboard

## 📖 Project Overview

This project focuses on optimizing inventory management and forecasting future demand for a retail business using Microsoft Excel.

The objective is to identify high-demand products, reduce excess inventory, prevent stockouts, and support data-driven inventory planning through demand forecasting and what-if analysis.

The dashboard provides business stakeholders with actionable insights into inventory performance, product demand, revenue contribution, and future inventory requirements.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

The company was facing:

- Excess inventory leading to high holding costs.
- Low-stock products causing potential stockouts.
- Difficulty identifying high-value products.
- Lack of demand forecasting for inventory planning.
- Uncertainty in inventory requirements under different demand scenarios.

Management required an analytical solution to optimize inventory levels and improve inventory planning decisions.

## 📈 Analysis Performed

### 1. Demand Analysis

- Category-wise demand analysis
- Top 10 products by units sold
- Bottom 10 products by units sold
- Region-wise demand analysis

### 2. ABC Analysis

Products were classified based on cumulative revenue contribution:

| Class | Revenue Contribution |
|---------|---------|
| A | Top 80% |
| B | Next 15% |
| C | Remaining 5% |

### 3. Inventory Health Analysis

Products categorized into:

- Optimal Stock
- Excess Stock
- Low Stock

Inventory value distribution analyzed across categories and stock status.

### 4. Demand Forecasting

Forecasted future demand using Excel Forecast Sheet to estimate expected monthly demand and confidence intervals.

### 5. Scenario Analysis

Evaluated inventory requirements under multiple demand scenarios:

| Scenario | Demand Change |
|------------|------------|
| Worst Case | -10% |
| Base Case | 0% |
| Best Case | +20% |

### 6. Goal Seek Analysis

Determined required sales volume to achieve target revenue growth using Excel Goal Seek.

## 🔍 Key Insights

- A-Class products contribute nearly 80% of total revenue.
- Approximately ₹32.46 Cr of inventory value is tied up in excess stock.
- Electronics and Fashion emerged as the highest-demand categories.
- Demand forecasting predicts stable monthly demand of approximately 16K units.
- Inventory requirements vary from 15.9K to 21.2K units depending on demand conditions.

---

## 💡 Recommendations

- Prioritize inventory planning for A-Class products.
- Reduce excess inventory in slow-moving categories.
- Improve replenishment strategies for high-demand products.
- Utilize demand forecasting for inventory planning.
- Continuously monitor inventory health to reduce carrying costs and stockout risks.
